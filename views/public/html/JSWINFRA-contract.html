All seafarers will be allowed to draw advances up to the amount of their earnings standing against their name. At no time will their accounts run into a negative balance. As far as possible, the company will endeavor to make advances available in U.S. Dollars / local currency.

CC)

ALLOTMENT:

The employer will provide the facility of sending one monthly allotment without any extra cost to the seafarer. You may leave the necessary instructions with the bank for the transfer of monies as required by you.

DD)

BALANCE OF WAGES:

As far as possible, the company will pay your balance of wages prior to your signing off the vessel. However, in the event of currency restrictions prevailing in that port, the employer will pay you in local currency if you desire so. If, in exceptional circumstances, funds are not available on board or with agents, the employer will settle your balance of wages within a month by remitting the same into your nominated bank account provided by you to the Master prior to signing off.

EE)

PROBATION PERIOD:

During your first contract with the employer, the first tour of duty will be considered as the probation period. In case a seafarer is promoted on board during their contract, the first three months after the promotion will be the probation period. During the probation period, the company is entitled to terminate your employment in case of unsatisfactory performance and appraisals from your superiors.

  • Rest Hours: 77 hours per week

You will be responsible for all personal taxation whether of an income or capital nature, which may be levied upon you from time to time. Form -16A will be issued by the employer for all tax deducted at the end of the financial year.

HH)

MEDICAL ATTENTION, SICK PAY, DEATH AND DISABILITY COMPENSATION:

  1. The employer will pay all medical expenses from the date of commencement of the contract until your repatriation to your town, for any sickness contracted or injury received whilst upon the employer’s business, provided it was not self-inflicted or due to your fault or negligence. A seafarer repatriated to their port of engagement, unfit due to sickness or injury, shall be entitled to medical attention (including hospitalization) at the company’s expense as per the MUI-ICCSA CBA (MIC) (Officers) or the NUSI-ICCSA CBA (NIC) (Ratings).
  2. Compensation for loss of life: In case of the death of a seafarer due to an accident while serving on board or while traveling to or from the vessel on the employer’s business or due to marine peril, the employer will pay compensation as per clause TT to their immediate next of kin.
  3. Disability Compensation: If a seafarer, due to no fault of their own, meets with an accident while working on board or while traveling to or from the vessel on the employer’s business or due to marine peril, and as a result, their ability to work is reduced, the employer will pay disability compensation as per clause UU.

II)

DENTAL / OPTICAL TREATMENT:

Cosmetic dental work will not be paid by the employer. Spectacles are to be at the seafarer’s own expense. In the event of spectacles being broken during service, replacements will be at the employer's expense (subject to a suitable on-board log entry).

JJ)

LOSS OF PERSONAL EFFECTS:

Seafarers shall be compensated for loss of personal effects resulting from fire, collision, sinking, or similar marine accidents, or occurring while they are being transported by rail, sea, or air (provided a claim for the lost baggage is made under Airline Rules with the airline at the time of recovery before leaving the airport) as unaccompanied baggage (excluding loss occasioned by the officer's own fault). The amount payable as compensation for loss of personal effects for officers shall be Rs. 75,000/- and for Ratings shall be Rs. 50,000/-. There will be no recovery in case of loss of valuables and cash.

KK)

TRAVEL:

Unless otherwise provided, the cost of travel from the country where the seafarer commenced services (the “Place of Engagement”) to the port of embarkation of the vessel and from the port of discharge to the Place of Engagement shall be at the employer’s expense. Seafarers will be flown economy class and will be entitled to baggage allowance as applicable. Any extra baggage expenses incurred will be paid by the seafarer. In case of domestic travel, other available modes such as road, rail, and water shall also be used depending on availability as per the employer. The terms and conditions of insurance cover applicable during service on board a vessel would apply from the time of the seafarer’s departure from their hometown to join the vessel and up to their return. This cover is subject to conditions under the repatriation clause of the agreement. Seafarers are advised to carry some currency in USD (approximately 50 USD) for international travel and in INR (approximately 2500 INR) for domestic travel to use in case of emergency during travel to the vessel. Any expenses incurred will be reimbursed upon submission of bills.

LL)

DOCUMENTS:

Seafarers are required to carry all necessary original documents for service on board the assigned vessel. Any expenses incurred by the employer due to non-compliance will be chargeable to the office. Such documents include:

  1. Valid Continuous Discharge Certificate (CDC)
  2. Passport with a validity of not less than 6 months, having at least 2 blank pages for any required visa.
  3. International vaccination certificate in respect of yellow fever and other specific requirements.
  4. Certificate of competency.
  5. Certificates of courses mandatory as per STCW’2010 convention.
  6. Certificate for any additional training attended by the seafarer.

MM)

DRUG & ALCOHOL:

At the time of joining the company, a seafarer is required to give an undertaking that he is not a habitual user of drugs other than those prescribed by a doctor. Seafarers’ attention is drawn to the abuse act as laid down by the company. Any infringements that incur a penalty or prosecution will not be defended by the employer. Seafarers must not consume alcohol within 4 hours of going on watch and must maintain a blood alcohol level of 0% (0 mg/l in breath). Violation of these requirements may lead to disciplinary action, including dismissal. The employer will not defend personal liability for any infringements of drug laws, and violations may result in termination of contract as per clause NN.

NN)

HEALTH, SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION POLICY:

Seafarers’ attention is drawn to the Manager’s policy on health, safety, and environmental protection, emphasizing priority for occupational health, safety at sea, prevention of human injury or loss of life, pollution prevention, and continual improvement in health, safety, and environmental performance.

OO)

EARLY TERMINATION OF CONTRACT:

A seafarer may resign at any time before the expiry of the contract by giving 1 (one) month’s notice in writing, in which case he will be entitled to leave pay pro-rata, but will be responsible for repatriation expenses. Leaving with less than 1 (one) month’s notice will also entitle the seafarer to leave pay pro-rata, but he will be responsible for repatriation and relief expenses, with the employer reserving the right to recover costs incurred. If employment is terminated due to circumstances beyond the seafarer’s control, the employer will provide 1 (one) month’s notice or wages in lieu, plus leave pay on a pro-rata basis and free repatriation. If discharged for misconduct or incompetence within this period, only wages up to the termination date will be provided, and the seafarer will be responsible for repatriation expenses. The company reserves the right to transfer the seafarer to any vessel managed by the company under similar terms and conditions.

SS)

SERVICE IN WAR ZONE OR WAR RISK AREAS (AS DECLARED BY LLOYD’S):

  1. A war risk bonus amounting to 100% of basic wage will be provided during service in a designated war risk area, starting from the day the ship enters the zone until it leaves, with a minimum payment of 5 days.
  2. In the event of death, the surviving kin will receive compensation of twice the amount they would have received had the vessel been trading in normal areas.
  3. In the event of disability, compensation will be twice the amount the officer would have received had the vessel been trading in normal areas.

TT)

MEDICAL and SICK PAY:

You warrant that you will disclose true and complete information about your health status during the medical examination. Any failure to disclose relevant information may result in liability for costs incurred by the employer. Sick pay is calculated at the rate of the seafarer’s basic pay. If a seafarer is landed at any port due to sickness or injury, he is entitled to sick pay plus guaranteed overtime until repatriated at the owner’s expense. Following repatriation, sick pay applies as follows:

  • In case of sickness, up to 16 weeks after discharge with valid medical certification.
  • In case of injury (not self-inflicted), for as long as medical attention is required or until a medical determination is made.

Seafarers must report to the manning agent for prompt medical attention upon repatriation. Failure to avail treatment may release the owner from liability. Satisfactory medical certificates, endorsed by the owner’s approved doctor, are required to prove entitlement to sick pay. If there is a disagreement, a third doctor may be jointly nominated by both parties, and their decision will be final.
