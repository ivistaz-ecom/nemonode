BALANCE OF WAGES:

As far as possible, the company will pay your balance of wages prior to your signing off the vessel. During the probation period, the company is entitled to terminate your employment in case of unsatisfactory performance and appraisals from your superiors.

MEDICAL ATTENTION, SICK PAY, DEATH AND DISABILITY COMPENSATION:

1. The employer will pay all medical expenses from the date of commencement of the contract until your repatriation to your town, for any sickness contracted or injury received whilst upon the employer’s business, provided it was not self-inflicted or due to your fault or negligence.

 

A seafarer repatriated to their port of engagement, unfit due to sickness or injury, shall be entitled to medical attention (including hospitalization) at the company’s expense as per the MUI-ICCSA CBA (MIC) (Officers) or the NUSI-ICCSA CBA (NIC) (Ratings).

 

2. Compensation for loss of life: In case of the death of a seafarer due to an accident while serving on board or while traveling to or from the vessel on the employer’s business or due to marine peril, the employer will pay compensation as per clause TT to their immediate next of kin.

 

3. Disability Compensation: If a seafarer, due to no fault of their own, meets with an accident while working on board or while traveling to or from the vessel on the employer’s business or due to marine peril, and as a result, their ability to work is reduced, the employer will pay disability compensation as per clause UU.

JJ)

LOSS OF PERSONAL EFFECTS:

Seafarers shall be compensated for loss of personal effects resulting from fire, collision, sinking, or similar marine accidents, or occurring while they are being transported by rail, sea, or air (provided a claim for the lost baggage is made under Airline Rules with the airline at the time of recovery before leaving the airport) as unaccompanied baggage (excluding loss occasioned by the officer's own fault). The amount payable as compensation for loss of personal effects for officers shall be Rs. 75,000/- and for Ratings shall be Rs. 50,000/-. There will be no recovery in case of loss of valuables and cash.

KK)

TRAVEL:

Unless otherwise provided, the cost of travel from the country where the seafarer commenced services (the “Place of Engagement”) to the port of embarkation of the vessel and from the port of discharge to the Place of Engagement shall be at the employer’s expense. Seafarers will be flown economy class and will be entitled to baggage allowance as applicable. Any extra baggage expenses incurred will be paid by the seafarer. In case of domestic travel, other available modes such as road, rail, and water shall also be used depending on availability as per the employer.

 

The terms and conditions of insurance cover applicable during service on board a vessel would apply from the time of the seafarer’s departure from their hometown to join the vessel and up to their return. This cover is subject to conditions under the repatriation clause of the agreement. Seafarers are advised to carry some currency in USD (approximately 50 USD) for international travel and in INR (approximately 2500 INR) for domestic travel to use in case of emergency during travel to the vessel. Any expenses incurred will be reimbursed upon submission of bills.

Company maintaining zero alcohol policy and at the time of joining crew were briefed compliance of drug and alcohol policy strictly.

OO)

EARLY TEMINATION OF CONTRACT:

A seafarer may resign from our services at any time before the expiry of his contract after giving 1(one)months’ notice in writing, in which case he will be entitled to leave pay-rata, but he is to be responsible for his repatriation expenses. In the event of his leaving the employers service on less that 1(one) months’ notice, he is entitled to leave pay pro-rata but he will be responsible for his repatriation and relief expenses and the employer reserves the right to recover from his all costs and / or losses incurred, which would not have arisen had he fulfilled his contract period.

 

If, due to circumstances involving no fault of a seafarer, it becomes necessary to terminate employment within the period of your service, the employer will give 1 (one) months’ notice in writing or 1 (one) month’s basic wages in lieu thereof, plus leave pay on pro-rata for the actual period of service and free repatriation to the place of your engagement.

 

If you are discharged due to your misconduct, negligence or incompetence within this period or during your probationary period as in (FF), you will receive wages only up to and including the day of termination of your services, but you will be responsible for your repatriation and relief expenses and for all costs and / or losses incurred, which would not have arisen, had you not been discharged.

 

The company reserves the right to transfer you on any vessel managed by the company during the tenure of this contract under similar terms and conditions.

SS)

SERVICE IN WAR ZONE OR WAR RISK LIKE AREAS ( AS DECLARED BY LLOYD’S:

If during the period of your employment, any area is designated as a war risk then you will during the period for which you are required to work in that area shall be entitled to following:

 

          1. A war risk bonus amounting to 100% of basic wage. Such entitlement to the bonus will start from and including the calendar day that the ship enters the zone and continue to and including the calendar day that the ship leaves the zone. However, the payment of bonus would not be less than 5 days.

          2. In the event of death, the surviving would receive a compensation of twice the amount they would have received had the vessel been trading in normal areas.

          3. In the event of disability, any compensation for disability would be twice the amount of compensation the officer would have received as if the vessel was trading in normal area.

 

TT)

MEDICAL and SICK PAY:

You warrant that you will disclose to the doctor, at the time of medical examination, true and complete information about and relevant to your past and present health status. Any continuing medication needed, and surgical operation undergone must be disclosed also. If you are subsequently found to have failed to disclose the true status of health, you will be liable for all costs and expenses incurred by the employer as a consequence thereof.

 

Sick Pay is calculated at the rate of seafarer’s Basic Pay.

 

If the seafarer is landed at any port because of sickness and injury, he is entitled to receive sick pay plus guaranteed overtime, or in case of officers, fixed overtime, until he has been repatriated at the owner’s expense. There after he is entitled to sick pay as follows:

 

In case of sickness up to 16 weeks after discharge, subject to submission of valid medical certificate, without undue delay.

 

In case of injury (other than self-inflicted injuries or injuries caused by willful act of the seafarer), for so long as medical attention is required or until a medical determination is made in accordance with clause TT concerning disability, whichever is sooner.

 

Provided however, that the seafarer reports as soon as possible upon repatriation to manning agent who will arrange such medical attention promptly. Where the nature of sickness or injury inhibits the seafarer from reporting to the manning agent it is incumbent upon the seafarer to notify them in writing accordingly so that alternative arrangements can be made for medical treatment. Failure by seafarer to avail himself of the treatment offered will release the owner from any liability to the seafarer in respect thereof.

 

The submission of satisfactory medical certificates endorsed, where necessary, by the owner’s approved doctor, shall be considered proof of continuing entitlement to sick pay. If a doctor appointed by on or behalf of the seafarer disagrees with the assessment, a third doctor may be jointly nominated by the owner and seafarer and the decision of this doctor will be final and binding on both parties.
